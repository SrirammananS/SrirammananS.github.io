PCI1A Theory Exam Answer Book (2019-2023)

PART A ANSWERS (2 marks, 50 words)

Basic Concepts & Definitions

1. Crime

  • Definition: Crime is a socially harmful act that violates established laws and is punishable by the state.
  • Elements: Actus reus (guilty act), Mens rea (guilty mind), causation, and harm to society.
  • Types: Felony (serious crimes) and Misdemeanor (minor crimes).

2. Tort

  • Definition: A civil wrong that causes harm or injury to another person, leading to legal liability.
  • Key Features: Breach of duty, causation of damage, remedy through compensation.
  • Examples: Negligence, defamation, trespass, assault.

3. Misdemeanour

  • Definition: Minor criminal offenses punishable by fines or imprisonment up to one year.
  • Characteristics: Less serious than felonies, local jurisdiction, summary trial.
  • Examples: Petty theft, simple assault, traffic violations.

4. Law

  • Definition: System of rules created and enforced by governmental institutions to regulate behavior.
  • Functions: Maintain order, resolve disputes, protect rights, punish wrongdoers.
  • Sources: Constitution, statutes, case law, regulations.

Cyber Crime Fundamentals

5. Cyber Crime

  • Definition: Criminal activities conducted using computers and internet to harm individuals or organizations.
  • Characteristics: Digital medium, global reach, anonymity, rapid execution.
  • Impact: Financial loss, data breach, privacy violation, psychological harm.

6. Cyber Criminology

  • Definition: Scientific study of cyber criminal behavior, causes, prevention, and social impact.
  • Focus Areas: Digital forensics, behavioral analysis, crime patterns, prevention strategies.
  • Importance: Understanding motives, developing countermeasures, policy formulation.

7. Cyber Space

  • Definition: Virtual environment created by interconnected computer networks where digital interactions occur.
  • Characteristics: Borderless, 24/7 accessibility, anonymity possible, rapid information flow.
  • Jurisdiction Issues: Cross-border crimes, legal complexities, enforcement challenges.

8. Cyber Criminals

  • Definition: Individuals or groups who commit illegal activities using digital technologies.
  • Types: Script kiddies, hacktivists, organized groups, nation-state actors.
  • Motivations: Financial gain, revenge, ideology, thrill-seeking.

9. Cyber Appellate Court

  • Definition: Specialized tribunal to hear appeals from Cyber Regulations Appellate Tribunal under IT Act 2000.
  • Jurisdiction: Cyber law violations, data protection disputes, digital evidence matters.
  • Powers: Review decisions, impose penalties, issue directions.

Contemporary Crime Forms

10. Contemporary Forms of Cybercrimes Against Property (4 types)

  1. Credit Card Fraud - Unauthorized use of payment cards.
  2. Online Banking Fraud - Illegal access to banking systems.
  3. E-commerce Fraud - Deceptive online business practices.
  4. Cryptocurrency Theft - Stealing digital currencies.

11. Forms of Organised Crimes (4 types)

  1. Human Trafficking - Illegal trade in persons.
  2. Drug Trafficking - International narcotics trade.
  3. Money Laundering - Converting illegal funds.
  4. Arms Trafficking - Illegal weapons trade.

12. Types of Cyber Crimes (4 types)

  1. Hacking - Unauthorized system access.
  2. Identity Theft - Stealing personal information.
  3. Phishing - Fraudulent information gathering.
  4. Malware Distribution - Spreading malicious software.

White Collar & Economic Crimes

13. WCC (White Collar Crime)

  • Definition: Non-violent crimes committed by business professionals using deception for financial gain.
  • Characteristics: High social status offenders, breach of trust, complex schemes.
  • Examples: Embezzlement, insider trading, tax evasion, corporate fraud.

14. GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance)

  • Governance: Corporate leadership and decision-making processes.
  • Risk: Identifying and managing potential threats to organization.
  • Compliance: Adhering to laws, regulations, and industry standards.
  • Importance: Prevent violations, reduce liability, maintain reputation.

15. Tax Evasion

  • Definition: Illegal practice of not paying taxes owed to government.
  • Methods: Underreporting income, false deductions, offshore accounts.
  • Consequences: Criminal prosecution, heavy fines, imprisonment.

16. Money Laundering

  • Definition: Process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate.
  • Stages: Placement, layering, integration.
  • Impact: Economic distortion, corruption, criminal enterprise funding.

Criminal Techniques & Methods

17. Modus Operandi

  • Definition: Habitual method or pattern used by criminals to commit offenses.
  • Importance: Crime solving, linking cases, predicting behavior, prevention strategies.
  • Elements: Approach method, attack pattern, escape route, victim selection.

18. DDOS Attack

  • Definition: Distributed Denial of Service - overwhelming target system with traffic.
  • Method: Multiple compromised systems attack single target simultaneously.
  • Impact: Service disruption, financial loss, reputation damage.

19. MIM Attack (Man-in-the-Middle)

  • Definition: Intercepting communication between two parties without their knowledge.
  • Techniques: Network sniffing, session hijacking, SSL stripping.
  • Prevention: Encryption, digital certificates, secure connections.

20. Hackers

  • Definition: Individuals with advanced computer skills who gain unauthorized access to systems.
  • Types: White hat (ethical), black hat (malicious), gray hat (mixed motives).
  • Skills: Programming, networking, system vulnerabilities, social engineering.

Criminological Theories & Theorists

21. Four Theories of Criminology Related to Cybercrime

  1. Differential Association Theory - Learning criminal behavior.
  2. Routine Activities Theory - Crime opportunity structure.
  3. Social Learning Theory - Modeling criminal behavior.
  4. Strain Theory - Gap between goals and means.

22. Sutherland's Contribution

  • White Collar Crime concept introduction.
  • Differential Association Theory development.
  • Crime as learned behavior emphasis.
  • Socioeconomic factors in crime causation.

23. Marcus Felson

  • Routine Activities Theory co-developer.
  • Crime triangle concept: motivated offender, suitable target, absence of guardian.
  • Environmental criminology contributions.
  • Crime prevention through environmental design.

24. Richard Cloward & Ohlin

  • Differential Opportunity Theory developers.
  • Legitimate and illegitimate opportunities concept.
  • Three delinquent subcultures: criminal, conflict, retreatist.
  • Social structure influence on crime patterns.

25. Suitable Target

  • Routine Activities Theory component.
  • Characteristics: Value, visibility, accessibility, inertia (VIVA).
  • Examples: Unguarded property, isolated individuals, valuable items.

26. Capable Guardian

  • Person or mechanism that prevents crime occurrence.
  • Types: Formal (police, security), informal (neighbors, family).
  • Effectiveness: Presence, vigilance, intervention capability.

Media & Behavioral Aspects

27. Media Influence on Human Behavior

  • Positive: Education, awareness, social cohesion, cultural preservation.
  • Negative: Violence modeling, unrealistic expectations, addiction, misinformation.
  • Mechanisms: Social learning, agenda setting, cultivation theory.

28. Social Media

  • Definition: Digital platforms enabling user-generated content and social interaction.
  • Crime Potential: Cyberbullying, identity theft, fake news, radicalization.
  • Benefits: Communication, networking, business opportunities, social movements.

Legal & Procedural Terms

29. FIR (First Information Report)

  • Definition: Written document by police recording first information about cognizable offense.
  • Copies Made: Original (police station), copy to complainant, copy to magistrate.
  • Importance: Investigation starting point, legal evidence, statutory requirement.

30. Charge Sheet

  • Definition: Formal accusation filed by police in court after investigation completion.
  • Contents: Accused details, offense description, evidence summary, witness list.
  • Timeline: Filed within 90 days for regular cases, 60 days for custody cases.

31. Cognizable Offence

  • Definition: Serious crimes where police can arrest without warrant.
  • Examples: Murder, rape, theft, cyber crimes.
  • Police Powers: Immediate arrest, investigation initiation, evidence collection.

32. Bail

  • Definition: Temporary release of accused with surety to ensure court appearance.
  • Types: Regular bail, anticipatory bail, default bail.
  • Conditions: Surety amount, reporting requirements, travel restrictions.

Crime Prevention & Control

33. Role of NGOs in Crime Prevention

  • Community Awareness: Education programs, workshops, campaigns.
  • Victim Support: Counseling, legal aid, rehabilitation.
  • Policy Advocacy: Law reform, rights protection, social justice.
  • Research: Crime analysis, prevention strategies, impact assessment.

34. Social Control

  • Definition: Mechanisms used by society to regulate behavior and maintain order.
  • Types: Formal (laws, police) and informal (family, community).
  • Functions: Prevent deviance, maintain stability, protect values.
